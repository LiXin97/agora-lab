# research-staff-1 -- Meeting M001 Judgment

## Overall Assessment

Both students have produced reasonable first-pass surveys, but neither is ready to ground a credible experimental comparison. Key gaps must be addressed before moving to experiment design.

## Concerns

1. **student-a**: Survey covers four models but omits important recent work (Based, GLA, DeltaNet). The comparison table lacks source citations, making numbers unverifiable. The proposed benchmark is too vague to execute.

2. **student-b**: Sparse attention survey is solid on 2020-2023 classics but thin on 2024-2025 developments. The "learnable sparsity" section covers only one paper. LongNet's 1B-token claim is presented without sufficient qualification.

## Quality Flags

- Neither survey includes a comparison table with cited sources
- Both proposed benchmarks overlap significantly (long-context retrieval) but neither student acknowledges the overlap or proposes coordination
- No discussion of compute budget constraints
- No discussion of model size -- are we comparing at 1.4B, 7B, or both?

## Judgment Recommendation

Both students should: (1) revise surveys with 2024-2025 literature, (2) add cited comparison tables, (3) agree on a shared evaluation protocol with explicit compute budget, and (4) define model sizes before designing experiments.
